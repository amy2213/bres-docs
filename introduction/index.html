    Introduction

    About the Author

    My name is Amy Laird, and I am the developer of the Binary Ritual Encoding System (BRES) — a new methodological framework for decoding symbolic manuscripts through a lens of ritual sequence, phase logic, and calendrical alignment.

    With a background spanning real estate operations, project management, and deep research into ancient manuscripts, I bring a hybrid of analytical precision and symbolic intuition. My ongoing work focuses on uncovering pattern-based systems embedded in undeciphered texts, beginning with the Voynich Manuscript, and now expanding into the Phaistos Disc, Dresden Codex, and other ancient symbolic artifacts.

    What is BRES?

    BRES (Binary Ritual Encoding System) is a ritual sequence classification system based on a four-phase logic: - Passive (P)
    - Transitional (T)
    - Active (A)
    - Neutral (N)

    These phases form the grammar of symbolic ritual sequences, allowing researchers to identify closure loops, transitional gates, and active invocations within undeciphered glyph-based texts. The BRES framework does not assume a linguistic cipher; rather, it interprets sequences as ceremonial instructions or encoded calendars.

    Why This Project?

    There has long been a gap in symbolic manuscript research: most interpretations assume either language-based encipherment or pure decoration. The BRES hypothesis proposes that many ancient artifacts encode ritual logic — not language, but a ritual calendar or process model.

    This project seeks to:

    • Prove that recurring glyph sequences match symbolic ritual structures.
    • Classify sectors by their ritual function using BRES logic.
    • Reveal calendrical structure in encoded artifacts.
    • Develop a toolkit for applying this framework to other undeciphered texts.

    My Hypothesis

    The central hypothesis is this:

    The Phaistos Disc, like the Voynich Manuscript and parts of the Dresden Codex, encodes a calendrical-ritual structure based on symbolic function, using repeated glyph sequences arranged in four-phase cycles of Passive, Transitional, Active, and Neutral operations.

    This method, if confirmed across multiple artifacts, may redefine how we interpret ancient symbolic systems. It suggests these objects were not meant to be read as texts, but to be enacted — as ritual calendars, ceremonial guides, or cosmological timers.

    Current Focus

    This documentation presents: - A full BRES classification of the Phaistos Disc. - A database of glyphs, sectors, and interpretations. - Technical components for the classifier, feature extractor, and use-case applications. - An expandable foundation for analyzing additional artifacts.
